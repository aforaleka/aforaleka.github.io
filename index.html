Last summer, I was in Cape Town, SA for the iXperience coding bootcamp. I spent my time there doing what I love -- code and travel. I learnt Ruby on Rails and built The Mudita Project. I also interned at GetSmarter, where I expanded its analytics to improve the learning experience of thousands of students.

During my time in Cape Town, I had the amazing opportunity to do things like bungee jumping, shark-cage diving, sandboarding and even walking with lions. But what made it even more special was that I got to talk to people living in townships and experience the other side of Cape Town. It has made me reflect on how lucky I am to have an education, and has encouraged me to pursue a career in software development in order to improve lives with technology.
